Snake Pit

Place snakes and identify their heads such that:

  • No snake orthogonally touches another snake of the same length
  • Snakes are one-cell-wide paths that do not touch themselves, even diagonally
  • The snakes have a head and a tail (there are no one-cell snakes)
  • No snake head can share an edge with another snake’s head or tail
  • Numbers present indicate the length of the snake in that cell
  • All cells are occupied by exactly one snake

Penguin Floes

Identify ice floes such that:

  • No floes share an edge
  • Each floe has exactly one number, and the number of cells in the floe is equal to that number
  • All water cells are edge-connected
  • No 2x2 square of water cells is present

At the same time, place penguins such that:

  • There is exactly one penguin on each ice floe
  • Each penguin is on the cell of its floe whose 8 surrounding cells contain the longest continuous stretch of water

Fish Tanks

  • Each region represents an aquarium that can be filled with water
  • Within an aquarium, the water reaches a certain number of cells up from its bottommost cell(s)
  • Shade cells to represent a water-filled cell, blank to represent an empty cell
  • Numbers on the outside represent the number of shaded/water-filled cells

At the same time, place fish in the tanks such that:

  • There are two fish in each aquarium, row, and column
  • All fish are within the water

Bat cave

  • Shade some cells to leave behind an unshaded cave
  • Numbers cannot be shaded
  • The cave is edge-connected
  • All shaded regions are edge-connected to the boundary
  • The number indicates the cells visible from there (horizontally and vertically, including self)

At the same time, place bats in the cave such that:

  • Each cell in the cave can be seen by at least one bat
  • No bat can see another bat
  • No bat occupies a number cell
